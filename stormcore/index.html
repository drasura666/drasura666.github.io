STORMCORE Reactor

Hybrid Framework for Artificial Lightning Energy Harvesting
Independent Research | July 2025 | Dr. Asura

Project Overview

The STORMCORE Reactor is a hybrid theoretical system that aims to generate and harvest artificial lightning through high-pressure steam cloud formation, ion separation, and capacitor-based energy conversion. It also proposes a natural lightning capture mechanism using grounded conductor towers and integrates all subsystems under a modular MULTIGEN power platform.

Core Components

  • Steam Cloud Chamber: High-temperature water forms convection clouds in a pressure-sealed reactor.
  • Hydraulic Compression: Pistons simulate vertical atmospheric forces.
  • Charge Stratification: Ion jets mimic natural cloud polarity separation.
  • Lightning Discharge: Triggered when electrostatic threshold is met.
  • Capacitor Capture: Discharge is routed to energy storage systems.

MULTIGEN System

The STORMCORE reactor feeds into a MULTIGEN power system capable of accepting mechanical, thermal, tidal, and electrical inputs. It can operate in remote or extreme environments by recycling steam, desalinating water, and buffering voltage through inverters and flywheel banks.

Engineering Limitations

  • Electrode degradation under repeated discharge
  • Maintaining ionic balance during cloud compression
  • Containment safety under plasma and arc load
  • EROI balancing with input-output energy costs
